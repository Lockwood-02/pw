# Cyberdeck
04-15-2026

Recently, I have fallen into the rabbit hole of **Cyberdecks**. These are devices that are basically portable computers that might serve a specific purpose. For instance, you might make a cyberdeck that is a **Linux** machine that is purely used for a 3d printer setup. This could be a tablet style creation or a station that is designed for this purpose. While totally working insanely hard at my job, I stumbled upon the [r/cyberdeck](https://www.reddit.com/r/cyberDeck/) subreddit. Needless to say, I was hooked. It also made me recall that my father had built one of these many years ago out of a **Pelican** case. This seemed to be the standard but I had no idea what it was at the time. He built it for portable gaming but ended up just selling it and buying a laptop instead. This, I think, is where the difference between enthusiast and casual comes into play. A casual might see a Cyberdeck and think, "well I'll just get a laptop", while an enthusiast would think, "woah I found an old altoids can. I can def make a pc out of this". They do it more for the love of the game, and I want to throw my hat in the ring. 

## Cyberdeck Ideas
Yesterday, a game called **Replaced** came out. In that game, there is an item called the **Wingman2**. It is a handheld that acts like our index and collectibles menu. Here is an image of the device:

![Wingman2](/images/Wingman2.jpg)

I personally LOVE the design of this tool and there are already some Cyberdecks that [you can purchase](https://www.clockworkpi.com/product-page/uconsole-kit-rpi-cm4-lite) that resemble the device pretty well. Initially, I didn't know what I would do with this type of device. Would it just be a portable pc that I would never use? Would it be my ereader for books? I wasn't sure at all. Then I got the idea of it being a portable terminal chat room device. Of course, it could serve multiple purposes but that idea hooked me more than the rest. Of course it could also be a portable hacking device (which I want to try to make as well), but that is for another time. 

This blog was more of a starting point to a yet to be started project. As I make progress on it, which will likely take a while to even begin, I will create more posts that break down the steps I took, the trials I endured, and the progress that has been made. But for now, that is all. I leave you with the concept.
